About the Employee Evaluation System

This system is designed to help employees and managers track performance, set goals, and facilitate professional development through regular evaluations.

How the Evaluation Process Works

1 Evaluation Periods

Evaluations are conducted during specific periods (quarterly, bi-annual, or annual). You'll receive notifications when a new evaluation period begins.

Tip: Check the dashboard to see the current evaluation period and deadlines.
2 Self-Evaluation

Employees complete a self-assessment by rating themselves on various competencies and providing comments about their performance.

  • Use the sliding scale (1-10) to rate each competency
  • Provide specific examples in the comments section
  • Be honest and objective in your assessment
3 Manager Evaluation

After you submit your self-evaluation, your manager will review it and provide their assessment of your performance.

Managers consider:

  • Your self-assessment ratings and comments
  • Their observations of your work
  • Feedback from colleagues and stakeholders
  • Your achievement of goals and objectives
4 Evaluation Review

Once your manager completes their evaluation, you'll have the opportunity to review their feedback and comments.

The review process includes:

  • Discussion of strengths and areas for improvement
  • Clarification of any points in the evaluation
  • Setting goals for the next evaluation period
5 Final Approval

After the review discussion, the evaluation is finalized and approved by both you and your manager. The completed evaluation becomes part of your performance record.

Frequently Asked Questions

The entire process typically takes 2-3 weeks from the start of the evaluation period to final approval. Self-evaluations should be completed within the first week, manager evaluations in the second week, and review meetings in the third week.

If you disagree with any aspect of your evaluation, you should first discuss it with your manager during the review meeting. If you're unable to resolve the disagreement, you can request HR mediation by contacting the HR department directly.

The overall score is a weighted average of your scores across all competencies. Each competency has a weight factor that reflects its importance for your specific role. The system automatically calculates your overall score and rating based on these weights.

Once submitted, you cannot directly edit your evaluation. However, if you need to make changes, you can request that your manager return it to you for revisions. This can be done through the "Request Revision" option if your evaluation is still in the review stage.

Your evaluation results are visible to you, your manager, and HR administrators. Senior leadership may have access to aggregated, anonymized data for reporting purposes, but individual evaluations are kept confidential within the management and HR teams.
